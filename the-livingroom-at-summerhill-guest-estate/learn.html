Notes from the table

Short reads on tasting menus, estate hospitality and the culture of a slow dinner — from The LivingRoom at Summerhill Guest Estate.

Common questions

What is a tasting menu?

A tasting menu is a fixed sequence of small courses served across an evening, designed to be eaten slowly rather than a single large plate — it's a format built for pace as much as flavour.

Do I need to dress up for fine dining at an estate restaurant?

Smart casual to elegant is usually a safe choice for estate fine dining — think a collared shirt or a dress rather than beachwear. If in doubt, ask when you reserve.

What's the benefit of a restaurant having its own garden?

A kitchen garden lets a menu follow the season closely and shortens the distance between picking and plating, which is why garden-forward kitchens often change their courses through the year.
