Notes from the chair

Grooming guides for Durban's Upper Highway — fades in the humidity, beard care between visits, and what to expect from a proper barbershop trip.

Common questions

How often should I get a haircut?

Most short back-and-sides styles hold their shape for 3–4 weeks before the fade grows out and the lines soften. Tighter fades and skin fades show growth faster and are usually best topped up every 2–3 weeks.

How do I keep a fade looking sharp between visits?

Brush hair in its natural direction daily to train it, use a light matte product rather than heavy grease which flattens the fade line, and avoid running clippers over it yourself — a fade is shaped in stages and is easy to flatten with a home trimmer.

What should I expect at my first visit?

A quick chat about the style and length you want, reference photos are always welcome, then the cut itself followed by a tidy-up of the neckline and edges. Ask questions during the cut — a good barber will happily adjust as they go.
