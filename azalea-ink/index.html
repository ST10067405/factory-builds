How to Care for Fresh Ink

Aftercare

Getting a tattoo is only half the story. What happens in the first two weeks determines how your piece heals, how vibrant the colour stays, and how cleanly the lines settle. At Azalea Ink, we give every client a full aftercare rundown, but here's the core of it.

Days 1–3: Protect. Keep the wrap on for the first few hours. When you remove it, wash gently with unscented soap and warm water. Pat dry, never rub. Apply a thin layer of unscented moisturiser or dedicated tattoo balm. Repeat twice daily.

Days 4–14: Heal. Your tattoo will peel and flake; this is normal. Do not pick or scratch. Keep it moisturised. Avoid direct sunlight, swimming pools, ocean water, and anything abrasive against the skin.

Long-term. SPF is your best friend. UV exposure fades ink faster than anything else. Once healed, apply sunscreen whenever the tattoo is exposed. A well-protected tattoo holds its detail for decades.

If you ever have questions about how your piece is healing, WhatsApp us, we're always here.

Choosing Where to Put Your Tattoo

Placement

Placement is one of the most important decisions in the tattoo process, and one of the most overlooked. It affects how the design reads, how it ages, the pain level, and whether you can conceal it when needed. Here's how we think about it at Azalea Ink.

Design follows form. The best tattoos work with the body's natural lines and contours. A piece designed for your forearm shouldn't just be slapped onto your calf; it should be reworked to flow with that placement.

Ageing matters. Areas that stretch, crease, or experience sun exposure age faster. Inner arm, ribs, and collarbone tend to hold detail well. Hands, fingers, and feet fade quickest.

Pain tolerance. Bony areas and zones close to nerves hurt more: ribs, spine, ditch of the elbow, tops of feet. Fleshier areas like outer thigh, upper arm, and calf are more comfortable. Your first tattoo? Go somewhere manageable.

Our consultations are free, come in, show us your idea, and we'll help you find the perfect placement.

Why Fine Line Tattoos Age the Way They Do

Style Guide

Fine line tattooing has exploded in popularity, and with it, a lot of confusion about what's realistic long-term. Here's an honest breakdown from the Azalea Ink studio.

The truth about thin lines. Fine line tattoos are beautiful precisely because of their delicacy. But delicate means they require exceptional technique to execute, and even then, they will soften over time. Lines spread slightly as the skin ages. This isn't failure; it's physics.

What affects longevity. Artist skill is the biggest factor: proper depth and needle choice matter enormously. Placement is second: areas that stretch or see friction fade faster. Aftercare is third: sun exposure accelerates fading significantly.

Choosing the right artist. Not every tattooist is trained in fine line work. Look for a healed portfolio, not just fresh photos. Ask how long they've been doing single-needle work. At Azalea Ink, we only take on work we're confident we can execute correctly.

Common Questions

Before You Book

Do you take walk-ins?+

No, all our work is by appointment only. This allows us to give each client our full attention and ensure your design is properly prepared. Book via WhatsApp.

How do I book a consultation?+

WhatsApp us on +27 66 547 2493 with a brief description of what you have in mind, your preferred placement, and any reference images. We'll set up a free consultation to discuss the design.

What's your deposit policy?+

We require a non-refundable deposit to secure your appointment. This deposit goes toward the cost of your tattoo. Deposits are forfeited if you cancel with less than 48 hours notice.

How much does a tattoo cost?+

Pricing is based on size, detail, placement, and style. Small pieces start from R800. Custom and large-scale work is quoted per piece. We never compromise on quality to cut costs; you get what you pay for with tattooing.

How should I prepare for my appointment?+

Eat a good meal beforehand. Stay hydrated. Wear loose, comfortable clothing that gives easy access to the area being tattooed. Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before. Don't come sunburned.

Can you do cover-up tattoos?+

Yes, we specialise in cover-up work. We'll need to assess the existing tattoo in person to advise on what's possible. Some pieces may require laser lightening sessions first. WhatsApp us with a photo for an initial assessment.

Do you do colour tattoos?+

Yes, we work in full colour, black and grey, and everything in between. Our neo-traditional style particularly lends itself to rich, long-lasting colour work.

Where exactly are you located?+

We're at 9 Old Main Road, Richdens Village Centre, Hillcrest, Durban. Parking is available in the centre. Look for the dark studio with the azalea logo in the window.
