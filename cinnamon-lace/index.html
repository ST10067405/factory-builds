Weddings

How to Choose Your Wedding Flowers

Your wedding flowers are one of the most personal elements of your day — they'll appear in every photograph, frame every memory, and travel with you down the aisle. Getting them right is about more than picking a colour.

Start With the Season

Flowers that are in season are fresher, more available, and more affordable. In Durban's subtropical climate, we have blooms available year-round — but summer brings proteas and tropical varieties, while autumn gifts us with lush peonies and garden roses.

Build Around One Hero Bloom

Choose one statement flower that speaks to you, then build complementary texture and colour around it. Peonies with eucalyptus. Roses with dried pampas grass. Ranunculus with soft ruscus. The hero does the talking; the supporting cast sets the scene.

Consider the Venue First

A garden ceremony calls for loose, romantic arrangements. A sleek ballroom suits sculptural, architectural flowers. Your venue's architecture, light and colour palette should guide the floral direction before your own preferences come in.

Seasonal

What's Blooming in Durban Right Now

One of the great joys of working with fresh flowers is that the palette changes with the seasons. Durban's warm, humid climate means something beautiful is always available — but what's at peak freshness shifts month to month.

Summer (Nov–Feb)

This is the season of tropical abundance. Strelitzias, ginger flowers, anthurium, and vivid proteas thrive in the heat. Colours run bold — deep reds, burnt oranges, electric yellows. Hydrangeas are available and lush.

Autumn & Winter (Mar–Aug)

As temperatures ease, so do the palettes. Roses, peonies, lisianthus and ranunculus come into their own. Soft blush, dusty mauve and warm champagne tones dominate. This is peak wedding season for a reason.

Spring (Sep–Oct)

The market explodes with tulips, anemones and garden carnations. Greenery is lush and varied. Spring weddings and events are some of our favourites to design — the mix of options is extraordinary.

Ask us what's freshest when you book. We design around what's truly exceptional that week, not what's on a fixed menu.

The Language of Flowers

What Your Favourite Bloom Really Means

Flowers have carried meaning for centuries — a language called floriography that allowed people to communicate feelings that words couldn't hold. When you choose a bloom, you're choosing a message.

The Rose

Red for passionate love. White for purity and new beginnings. Blush for gratitude and admiration. Pink for joy. Choosing a rose is choosing the shade of feeling you want to send.

The Peony

In Eastern tradition, peonies represent prosperity and good fortune. In the West, they're the flower of romance and bashfulness. We think they're simply the most luxurious bloom in existence.

The Protea

South Africa's national flower speaks of diversity, change, and courage. It's bold, architectural, and unmistakably us — we love including South African varieties in local events.

Lavender

Calm, serenity, devotion. Lavender is perfect for spaces where you want to soften the energy — a boardroom arrangement, a bedroom posy, or a late-afternoon wedding ceremony.

When we design for you, we think about meaning as much as aesthetics. Your flowers should feel intentional.

Care Tips

5 Tips to Keep Your Bouquet Fresh Longer

Fresh flowers are a living thing — with a little care, you can extend their life significantly. Here's what we tell every client when they leave with an arrangement.

1. Cut the Stems Immediately

As soon as you get home, recut each stem at a 45-degree angle underwater. This removes the air bubble that forms and allows the flower to drink properly. Repeat every two days.

2. Use the Right Water Temperature

Most flowers prefer lukewarm water — not cold, not hot. The exception is bulb flowers like tulips, which prefer cold. Add a sachet of flower food if one was provided.

3. Keep Away From Heat & Direct Sun

The enemy of fresh flowers is warmth. Avoid placing arrangements near sunny windows, above heat vents, or next to fruit bowls (ethylene gas from ripening fruit accelerates wilting).

4. Remove Dying Leaves & Petals

As individual blooms or leaves begin to fade, remove them. They introduce bacteria into the water that shortens the life of everything around them.

5. Mist Delicate Petals

For peonies, roses and ranunculus, a light daily mist on the petals helps them stay open and hydrated. Don't overdo it — just a fine spray each morning.

Trends

Trending Floral Styles for 2026 Events

The world of event florals is always moving — and 2026 has brought some of the most interesting directions we've seen in years. Here's what's defining the aesthetic right now.

Abundance, Not Minimalism

The tight, spherical arrangements of the 2010s have given way to wild, abundant designs that feel like they've just been cut from a garden. Overflowing, asymmetrical, layered — the look is intentionally imperfect.

Earth Tones & Moody Palettes

Burnt terracotta, deep burgundy, chocolate, and dusty sage have overtaken the blush-and-white aesthetic. Events feel grounded, warm, and cinematic. We're loving it.

Dried & Fresh Mixed

Pampas grass, dried proteas, and preserved eucalyptus mixed with fresh blooms adds texture and longevity to arrangements. The contrast creates something timeless.

Structural, Architectural Centrepieces

Tall, dramatic arrangements that create theatre at the dinner table — sculptural branches, cascading greenery, dramatic focal blooms. The centrepiece as conversation piece.

Behind the Blooms

A Day in Our Windermere Studio

Most people see the finished arrangement. Few see what goes into it. Here's a glimpse into our studio — the early starts, the decisions, the details that make Cinnamon & Lace what it is.

4:30am — The Market Run

The flower market in Durban opens before dawn. We arrive early to get first pick of the freshest stems — roses still tightly budded, peonies just beginning to open, greenery still dewy from overnight cold storage.

7:00am — Studio Setup

Back at the studio, we sort, recut and condition every stem before it's arranged. Flowers need time to rest in water after transport. We use this time to plan the day's designs.

9:00am — Arranging Begins

The best part. Each arrangement is built by hand — no floristry foam, no shortcuts. We work intuitively, adjusting height, direction and texture as we go.

12:00pm — Client Pickups & Deliveries

Fresh arrangements go out to clients across Durban. There's something deeply satisfying about delivering flowers — the reaction on someone's face when they open the door is worth every early morning.
