Common questions

How often should I have a check-up?

Most dentists recommend a check-up and clean every six months, though some patients need more frequent visits depending on their oral health. Your dentist can advise a schedule that suits you.

Why do my gums bleed when I brush?

Bleeding gums are often an early sign of gum inflammation (gingivitis), usually caused by plaque build-up along the gumline. It's common, but it's worth mentioning at your next check-up rather than ignoring it.

What can I do about sensitive teeth?

Tooth sensitivity can come from worn enamel, gum recession, or a cavity. A desensitising toothpaste can help day to day, but a dentist should check the underlying cause.
