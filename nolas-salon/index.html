Nail Care

How to Make Your Gel Nails Last 3–4 Weeks

Gel nails should last 3–4 weeks with the right prep and aftercare. Most sets that fail early come down to one of three things: poor prep, wet hands, or picking habits.

The prep stage is everything. Clean, dry, unpolished nails with proper cuticle work are the foundation. If there's moisture or oil on the nail plate when the gel goes on, it won't bond properly — and you'll start getting lift within days.

The first 24 hours matter most. Avoid submerging your hands for extended periods right after a gel service. Washing dishes with rubber gloves, avoiding hot showers immediately after — small things that let the gel fully cure and bond.

Cuticle oil daily. This one surprises clients, but moisturised nail beds actually extend gel life. Dry skin around the nail causes the gel to lift from the sides. Apply cuticle oil every night — your nails will thank you.

Never pick or peel. Peeling gel rips layers off your nail plate and causes the kind of damage that takes months to grow out. If your set is ready to come off, book a removal appointment at Nolas Salon — 072 128 9685.

With proper aftercare and our quality gel products, most clients come back for a fill at 3 weeks with the base still perfectly intact.

Nail Art

Choosing the Right Nail Shape for Your Hand Type

The shape of your nail set changes how your fingers look more than any colour or design. Here's a quick guide to the six main shapes and who they suit.

Oval — Universally flattering. Extends the appearance of shorter fingers. The go-to for clients who want an elegant, elongated look without going too dramatic. Works on almost any hand type.

Almond — Similar to oval but with a more pointed tip. Very feminine and fashion-forward. Best on longer nail beds — if your natural nails are very short, almond extensions will need extra length.

Square — Clean, modern, professional. Great for wider nail beds. Keeps the nail's natural width all the way to the top. Practical for clients who use their hands at work.

Coffin (Ballerina) — Long, flat-top, tapered sides. Dramatic and on-trend. Requires longer nails or extensions to look right. Popular for nail art because of the wide canvas.

Stiletto — The most dramatic. Very pointed tip. Makes fingers look slender and long. Not practical for daily tasks — but perfect for a photo shoot or special event.

Squoval — The hybrid between square and oval. Square sides with gently rounded corners. Great if you like square but find the sharp corners snag. Very low-maintenance.

Bridal

Bridal Beauty Timeline: When to Book Each Treatment

Wedding day beauty takes planning — not just the day of, but the months before. Here's the exact timeline we recommend to every bride.

6 months out: Book trials. Your makeup trial, hair trial, and nail consultation should all happen 6 months before the wedding. This gives time to adjust — if a look isn't right, you have months, not days, to find something better.

3–4 months out: Start skin prep. Whatever treatments you want for your face — facials, chemical peels, brow shaping — start now. Skin takes time to respond. You want your skin at its best 2 weeks before the wedding, not the day of.

6 weeks out: Confirm all bookings. Check that your nail, hair and makeup artists have your wedding date locked in. Arrange to pay deposits. Confirm your bridal party numbers.

2 weeks out: Last colour and cut. Hair colouring and cutting should happen 2 weeks before — this gives roots time to settle and any colour to tone down from fresh-vibrant to natural-beautiful.

3–5 days before: Waxing. Any body waxing should happen 3–5 days before the wedding so the skin has time to calm down and any redness fades.

Day before: Fresh nails. Get your gel manicure the day before — it'll be perfectly set by the morning of the wedding. Call 082 886 6694 to book your bridal package with us.

Hair

Balayage vs. Highlights: Which Colour Technique Suits You?

Both balayage and highlights add dimension and lightness to your hair — but they work differently, grow out differently, and suit different lifestyles. Here's how to choose.

Traditional highlights use foils to lighten individual sections of hair from root to tip. The result is uniform, bright, and high-contrast. Highlights give you crisp lightness through the hair and grow out with a noticeable root line, meaning you typically need a touch-up every 6–8 weeks.

Balayage is painted freehand onto the hair surface — the colour is concentrated at the mid-lengths and tips with a soft, blended root. The grow-out is gradual and natural, and many clients can go 4–6 months between appointments without the harsh regrowth line of foil highlights.

Choose highlights if: you want maximum lightness, bright all-over dimension, or a more structured, polished look. Good for clients who book regular maintenance.

Choose balayage if: you want a low-maintenance, sun-kissed look that grows out beautifully. Great for clients who don't want to be at the salon every 6 weeks.

Studio Life

Why Appointments-Only Salons Deliver Better Results

Walk-in salon culture prioritises volume. Appointments-only culture prioritises quality. The difference in what you experience is significant.

Your appointment is protected time. At a walk-in salon, your nail tech may have started your set already planning when they need to move on to the next client. At an appointments-only studio, your time is allocated specifically to you — nothing is rushed.

Preparation is tailored to you. Because we know who's coming in and when, we can prepare the products, colours and tools for your specific service. No grabbing whatever's available at the station.

The specialist knows your history. When you book consistently with the same specialist, they learn your nails, your hair, your skin. They know what's worked, what caused lifting last time, what you're trying to grow out. That context makes every visit better than the last.

No waiting. Walk-in salons will have you waiting 30–45 minutes at peak times. At Nolas Salon, your appointment starts on time. We respect your schedule.

Trends

Nail Art Trends in Westville This Season

At Nolas Salon, we see what Westville clients are asking for every week. Here's a breakdown of the sets that are dominating our appointment book right now.

Quiet luxury neutrals. Milky whites, barely-there pinks, soft beige with gold accents. This aesthetic is about restraint and elegance — perfect for clients who want beautiful nails that work in professional settings. The gold detail is usually a single nail or subtle line, not all-over chrome.

Detailed florals. Hand-painted flowers — cherry blossoms, daisies, roses — on almond or coffin shapes. Usually done on an accent nail or two, with the remaining nails in a complementary solid. The detail level at Nolas Salon means no two sets look the same.

French art variations. The classic French tip has been completely reinvented. Coloured tips (burgundy, teal, dusty rose), reverse French, bubble tips, wavy lines in place of straight tips. French without being basic.

Gem and crystal sets. Swarovski-style gems used as focal points — single nails encrusted in gems while the rest are minimal. Or gem-outlined designs over a neutral base. Great for events, birthdays, or just when you want your hands to get noticed.

Marbled effects. White marble with gold veining on a long oval or coffin shape. A timeless look that hasn't slowed down — and at Nolas, the marble effect is done by hand, not with a sticker.
