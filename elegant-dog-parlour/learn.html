Coat Care & Grooming Notes

Practical guidance for Westville dog owners — written for pet parents in Dawncliffe and across the Upper Highway.

Common questions

How can I tell if my dog has a double coat?

Double-coated breeds have a soft, insulating undercoat beneath a coarser topcoat. If you part the fur and see a dense, fluffy layer close to the skin, your dog likely has a double coat — common in breeds like Labradors, Huskies, Corgis and Border Collies.

How should I prepare my dog before a grooming appointment?

A quick brush-through at home to loosen surface tangles, a toilet break beforehand, and arriving a few minutes early so your dog can settle in all help a grooming visit go smoothly.

What should I expect at my dog's first grooming visit?

Expect an unhurried introduction, a check of coat and skin condition, and a groom paced to your dog's comfort. It's normal for a first visit to take a little longer than routine ones.
