How to Ask for the Right Fade

Cuts

"Just give me a fade" is the fastest way to end up with something you didn't want. Here's the vocabulary that gets you exactly the cut you're picturing.

Height. A low fade keeps the blend close to the ears, a mid fade sits around temple height, and a high fade pushes the blend up near the top of the head for maximum contrast.

Finish. A skin fade (or "bald fade") blends all the way down to skin. A taper keeps a bit more length at the bottom for a softer, less dramatic finish.

Top length. Fades are only half the story: tell us how much length you want on top, and whether you want texture, a side part, or something slicked back.

Still not sure? Bring a photo. We'll talk you through what'll actually work for your hair type and face shape before we pick up the clippers.

Colour Care 101

Colour

Fresh colour looks incredible on day one; the trick is keeping it that way. Here's what our colourists actually recommend.

Wait before washing. Hold off on shampooing for at least 48 hours after a colour service. This gives the cuticle time to fully close and locks the colour in.

Go sulphate-free. Sulphates strip colour fast. A good sulphate-free shampoo and conditioner will noticeably extend how long your colour stays true.

Cold water, less heat. Hot water and heat styling both accelerate fading. Rinse on cooler settings where you can, and use a heat protectant before any styling tools.

Ask us about our in-salon colour care range at your next visit, we'll match it to exactly what you had done.
